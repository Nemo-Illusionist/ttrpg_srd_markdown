# Glossary

## Abilities

| Term | Description |
|---|---|
| Charisma (CHA) | Measures force of personality, persuasiveness, and leadership |
| Constitution (CON) | Measures health, stamina, and vital force |
| Dexterity (DEX) | Measures agility, reflexes, and balance |
| Intelligence (INT) | Measures mental acuity, information recall, and analytical skill |
| Strength (STR) | Measures physical power and carrying capacity |
| Wisdom (WIS) | Measures awareness, intuition, and insight |

## Conditions

| Condition | Description |
|---|---|
| Blinded | Cannot see; attack rolls against have Advantage, own attack rolls have Disadvantage |
| Charmed | Cannot attack or target the charmer with harmful effects; charmer has Advantage on social checks |
| Deafened | Cannot hear; automatically fails checks that require hearing |
| Exhaustion | Subtract Exhaustion level from D20 Tests and Spell save DCs; die at 10 levels |
| Frightened | Disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while source of fear is in line of sight; cannot willingly move closer to source |
| Grappled | Speed becomes 0; ends if grappler is Incapacitated or moved out of reach |
| Incapacitated | Cannot take actions, Bonus Actions, or Reactions |
| Invisible | Impossible to see without special sense or magic; attack rolls against have Disadvantage, own attack rolls have Advantage |
| Paralyzed | Incapacitated; cannot move or speak; auto-fail Strength and Dexterity saves; attacks have Advantage, melee hits are Critical Hits |
| Petrified | Transformed to inanimate substance; Incapacitated, unaware; attack rolls against have Advantage; auto-fail Strength and Dexterity saves |
| Poisoned | Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks |
| Prone | Disadvantage on attack rolls; melee attacks against have Advantage, ranged attacks have Disadvantage; standing costs half movement |
| Restrained | Speed becomes 0; attack rolls against have Advantage, own attack rolls have Disadvantage; Disadvantage on Dexterity saves |
| Stunned | Incapacitated; cannot move; can speak only falteringly; auto-fail Strength and Dexterity saves; attacks have Advantage |
| Unconscious | Incapacitated; cannot move or speak; unaware; drops held items and falls Prone; auto-fail Strength and Dexterity saves; attacks have Advantage, melee hits are Critical Hits |

## Damage Types

| Damage Type | Description |
|---|---|
| Acid | Corrosive spray, digestive enzymes, and similar |
| Bludgeoning | Blunt force from hammers, falling, constriction |
| Cold | Infernal chill from frost and ice |
| Fire | Flames, dragon breath, and extreme heat |
| Force | Pure magical energy |
| Lightning | Bolts of electricity |
| Necrotic | Life-draining energy from undead and dark magic |
| Piercing | Puncturing from arrows, fangs, and spears |
| Poison | Venomous stings and toxic gases |
| Psychic | Mental assault that disrupts the mind |
| Radiant | Holy light and searing luminance |
| Slashing | Cutting from swords, claws, and axes |
| Thunder | Concussive sonic energy |

## Creature Types

| Creature Type | Description |
|---|---|
| Aberration | Utterly alien beings, such as aboleths and cloakers |
| Beast | Non-Humanoid natural creatures, like horses and wolves |
| Celestial | Magical creatures with ties to the Upper Planes, such as angels and pegasi |
| Construct | Magically created creatures, such as homunculi and shield guardians |
| Dragon | Scaly beings of ancient origin, such as red dragons and wyverns |
| Elemental | Beings from the Elemental Planes, such as efreet and water elementals |
| Fey | Creatures tied to the Feywild or the forces of nature, such as dryads and goblins |
| Fiend | Creatures tied to the Lower Planes, such as balors and hell hounds |
| Giant | Towering beings with humanlike shapes, like fire giants and trolls |
| Humanoid | People defined by their roles and professions, including varied species |
| Monstrosity | Unnatural creatures with strange origins, such as mimics and owlbears |
| Ooze | Gelatinous creatures, including black puddings and gelatinous cubes |
| Plant | Sentient vegetation and fungal monsters, such as shambling mounds and treants |
| Undead | Spirits and the reanimated dead, such as ghosts, vampires, and zombies |

## Keywords and Mechanics

| Term | Description |
|---|---|
| Action | The main thing a creature does on its turn (Attack, Cast a Spell, Dash, etc.) |
| Advantage | Roll two d20s and use the higher roll |
| Area of Effect | A region (Cone, Cube, Cylinder, Emanation, Line, Sphere) affected by a spell or ability |
| Armor Class (AC) | A number representing how hard a creature is to hit with an attack |
| Attack Roll | A d20 roll to determine if an attack hits a target |
| Attunement | A magical bond between a creature and a magic item (max 3 items) |
| Blindsight | Perceive surroundings without relying on sight within a specific radius |
| Bloodied | A creature is Bloodied when at half its Hit Points or fewer |
| Bonus Action | An additional action on your turn, granted by specific features or spells |
| Cantrip | A spell that can be cast at will without using a spell slot |
| Challenge Rating (CR) | A rating indicating how dangerous a monster is |
| Concentration | Maintaining focus on a spell; broken by damage, another Concentration spell, or Incapacitation |
| Cover | Physical protection from attacks and effects (Half, Three-Quarters, Total) |
| Critical Hit | Rolling a 20 on an attack roll; deal extra damage |
| D20 Test | An ability check, attack roll, or saving throw |
| Damage Roll | A roll to determine how much damage an attack or effect deals |
| Darkvision | See in darkness as if Dim Light, and Dim Light as if Bright Light, within a range |
| Death Saving Throw | A special save made when at 0 Hit Points to determine survival |
| Difficult Terrain | Terrain that costs extra movement to traverse (1 extra foot per foot moved) |
| Difficulty Class (DC) | The target number for a saving throw or ability check |
| Disadvantage | Roll two d20s and use the lower roll |
| Encounter | A scene in an adventure (combat, social, exploration) |
| Exhaustion | A debilitating condition tracked in levels that penalizes D20 Tests |
| Experience Points (XP) | Points earned by overcoming challenges, used to gain levels |
| Expertise | Double the Proficiency Bonus for a specific skill or tool check |
| Grappling | Seizing a creature using the Grapple option of an Unarmed Strike |
| Healing | Restoring Hit Points to a creature |
| Heroic Inspiration | Grants Advantage on one D20 Test; gained from class features and DM awards |
| Hit Point Dice (Hit Dice) | Dice representing a creature's health reserve, used during Short Rests |
| Hit Points (HP) | A numerical measure of a creature's vitality |
| Immunity | Complete protection against a damage type or condition |
| Initiative | Determines the order of turns in combat |
| Long Rest | A period of at least 8 hours of rest that restores Hit Points and resources |
| Magical Effect | Any effect from a spell, magic item, or magical feature |
| Multiattack | A monster ability allowing multiple attacks in one Attack action |
| Opportunity Attack | A Reaction melee attack against a creature leaving your reach |
| Passive Perception | 10 + Wisdom (Perception) modifier; used for noticing things without a check |
| Proficiency Bonus | A bonus added to D20 Tests based on character level (+2 to +6) |
| Reaction | An instant response to a trigger (one per round) |
| Resistance | Take half damage from a specific damage type |
| Ritual | A spell cast with extra time (10 minutes) without expending a spell slot |
| Saving Throw | A d20 roll to resist a spell, trap, poison, or similar effect |
| Short Rest | A period of at least 1 hour of rest during which Hit Dice can be spent |
| Speed | The distance in feet a creature can move on its turn |
| Spell Attack | An attack roll using spellcasting ability modifier + Proficiency Bonus |
| Spell Save DC | 8 + spellcasting ability modifier + Proficiency Bonus |
| Spell Slot | A resource expended to cast a spell of level 1 or higher |
| Surprise | A state where caught-off-guard creatures have Disadvantage on Initiative |
| Telepathy | A magical ability to communicate mentally within a specified range |
| Temporary Hit Points | Extra Hit Points that serve as a buffer; don't stack |
| Tremorsense | Detect creatures and objects through vibrations on the same surface |
| Truesight | See through magical and nonmagical Darkness, Invisibility, illusions, and shape-shifting |
| Unarmed Strike | A melee attack with a body part (fist, head, etc.) dealing 1 + Strength modifier Bludgeoning damage |
| Vulnerability | Take double damage from a specific damage type |
